# Guide: References and Citation System

## What this guide does

This guide explains what the references section actually contains and what kind of source base the thesis relies on. It is a reading companion to the bibliography, not only a checking list.

## References in one simple view

- The reference list combines standards, vendor documentation, cloud documentation, academic articles, books, conference papers, and technical reports.
- The thesis relies heavily on four source families.
- First, 3GPP and other standards sources.
- Second, vendor and platform documentation for the UAV, Dock, cloud, and communication environment.
- Third, academic research on UAVs, smart cities, cloud systems, 5G, and cybersecurity.
- Fourth, implementation-oriented documentation for Azure and related services.
- In simple words, the thesis is built from both scholarly literature and operational documentation.

## Detailed reading guide

### Standards and normative sources

- A large part of the bibliography is made of standards and official specifications.
- The strongest standards presence is 3GPP, covering UAV support, 5G system architecture, procedures, security architecture, restoration, and study reports.
- NIST SP 800-53 is used as the main general control catalog.
- ISO or IEC material appears for smart-city architecture and cloud-security or privacy controls.
- CSA material is also part of the standards base later in the thesis.
- In simple words, these sources give the thesis its formal security-governance backbone.

### Vendor and platform documentation

- Another large block of references comes from platform vendors and service providers.
- The UAV platform and Dock are documented through enterprise UAV vendor materials.
- The command-and-control platform and cloud deployment are supported by vendor deployment manuals and cloud API tutorials.
- Azure services such as virtual machines, storage, VPN, firewall, front door, functions, monitoring, key vault, and private networking are documented through Microsoft's official material.
- In simple words, these references explain how the real case architecture works in practice.

### Academic literature on UAV use and smart-city context

- The references also include literature on drone use in emergency response, surveillance, infrastructure inspection, environmental monitoring, logistics, healthcare, and smart-city systems.
- These sources are used to justify why UAVs matter operationally and why they are increasingly part of urban digital infrastructure.
- This is the background layer behind the motivation and theoretical chapters.

### Academic literature on cybersecurity and networking

- The bibliography also includes literature on 5G security, UAV security, attack patterns, cloud robotics, and cyber-physical systems.
- These sources support the threat analysis, attack logic, and conceptual framing of the thesis.
- They help connect the practical case study to broader research debates and known attack patterns.

### What the reference mix tells you about the thesis

- The thesis is not purely theoretical.
- It depends strongly on implementation documentation because the architecture is platform-specific.
- It is also not only a vendor-based technical report.
- It uses academic and standards sources to justify the threat model and the standards gap analysis.
- In simple words, the bibliography shows a hybrid thesis: part engineering case study, part standards analysis, part cybersecurity research.

### Main structural patterns inside the bibliography

- Early entries are dominated by architecture-building sources such as 3GPP, UAV documentation, cloud deployment material, and Azure services.
- The middle section broadens into smart-city and UAV-use literature.
- Later parts support theoretical security concepts, threat modeling, and standards mapping.
- This means the reference list mirrors the thesis structure itself.

### Main weaknesses this kind of bibliography can easily produce

- Heavy use of vendor documentation can create duplication if the same platform source is entered several times in slightly different forms.
- Standards entries can easily drift in year or version across chapters.
- Official web documentation can create inconsistent author fields and inconsistent internet citation formatting.
- Mixed source types can make the bibliography look uneven if style is not controlled carefully.

## Quick recap

- The reference list is the evidence base of the thesis.
- It combines standards, vendor documents, cloud documentation, and academic research.
- The standards sources support the gap analysis.
- The vendor and cloud sources support the reference architecture.
- The academic sources support the smart-city, UAV, security, and threat-analysis arguments.
- If this references section is read correctly, the reader should understand what bodies of evidence the thesis stands on and why consistency here matters for the credibility of the whole work.
