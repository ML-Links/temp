# Chapter 7: Summary


## Overall assessment

Chapter 7 is readable and covers all the major parts of the thesis. The structure is clear: problem and motivation, research methodology, principal findings, contributions, stakeholder implications, and concluding remarks. A reader who only reads this chapter will understand what the thesis is about.

The chapter's problem is that it does not yet behave like a summary. 

1. It is argumentative, evaluative, and partly persuasive in tone. These qualities belong to Chapter 6. 
2. Section 7.4 contains detailed stakeholder recommendations that effectively repeat Chapter 6's framework and organizational controls in a different register. 
3. Section 7.5 ("Concluding Remarks") is openly rhetorical in places. The chapter also makes several strong claims that go beyond what the thesis's analytical evidence supports.

> After Chapter 6 is revised (as described in the Chapter 6 feedback), Chapter 7 should be revisited to align with its revised role: a concise, factual recap of the completed research.

---

## What works well

- The four-phase methodology is accurately summarised in Section 7.2.2.
- The principal findings (7.2.3) clearly identify the most critical threats and the gap themes — this is factual and appropriate for a summary.
- The five contributions (Section 7.3) are stated concisely and match the thesis's actual deliverables.
- The chapter structure is easy to follow.

---

## Issues to address

### 1. Remove or condense the stakeholder implications section

**What is wrong:** Section 7.4 ("Implications for Stakeholders") provides detailed, paragraph-length guidance for urban planners, UAV operators, and cybersecurity professionals. This guidance largely repeats the organisational controls (O-1 through O-8) and framework recommendations from Chapter 6.

**Why it matters:** The summary template expects a concise, factual recap, not a new layer of recommendations. Extended stakeholder guidance in a summary chapter weakens the structural distinction between Chapter 6 (where those recommendations belong) and Chapter 7.

**How to fix it:** Condense Section 7.4 to two or three sentences that state who the thesis is relevant to and why, without repeating the full control recommendations. For example:

> *"The findings are directly relevant to three stakeholder groups. Urban planners and emergency-response organisations can use the framework ... Cybersecurity professionals and standards bodies can ... Detailed guidance for each group is provided in Chapter 6."*

---

### 2. Tone down the rhetorical register of Section 7.5

**What is wrong:** Section 7.5 ("Concluding Remarks") contains rhetorical statements that are persuasive rather than factual:

- *"The work does not claim to make a 5G‑UAV system impervious to attack. No such claim can
be honestly made for any complex cyber‑physical system."* This defensive framing is appropriate for a limitations section, not a summary's closing.
- *"It is the author’s hope that they will
contribute to a future in which smart‑city UAV services are deployed with the confidence that
their cyber foundations are as robust as the physical aircraft that carry them."* This is introduces personal tone.
- *"that shift is not merely an academic achievement; it is a prerequisite for the safe and responsible use of aerial robotics in the service of human life."* This is an evaluative claim about the societal significance of the work, not a factual summary.

**Why it matters:** A summary chapter's final paragraph should state what the thesis accomplished and close factually. 

**How to fix it:** Replace the closing paragraph of Section 7.5 with a concise factual summary

---

### 3. Shorten the chapter overall

**What is wrong:** The chapter is long enough to function as a second discussion chapter. Several subsections repeat content from Chapters 5 and 6 at a level of detail that is not appropriate for a summary.

**Why it matters:** The summary should give the reader a quick, high-level recap. If it is as detailed as the chapters it summarizes, it adds length without adding insight.

**How to fix it:** After revising Sections 7.4 and 7.5 as described above, run one more shortening pass:

- Section 7.2.3 (Principal Findings): Keep the threat-landscape and gaps-in-standards subsections, but reduce the framework-and-risk-reduction description to 3–4 sentences.
- Section 7.3 (Contributions): This section is already concise. no changes needed
- Target overall chapter length: approximately one-third shorter than the submitted version.

---

### 4. Consider revisiting Chapter 7 after the Chapter 5/6 restructuring is complete

If the framework is moved from Chapter 6 to Chapter 5 (as recommended in the Chapter 5 and Chapter 6 feedback), the summary chapter references to "Chapter 6 framework" will need to be updated to "Chapter 5 framework." 

The final editing of Chapter 7 should be done after the Chapter 5/6 restructuring is complete to avoid re-editing the same content twice.
